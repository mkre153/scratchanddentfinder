## Full Outline

### 1. How Yale Tracks Reliability

Yale Appliance is unique in the industry — they track every service call across their 40,000+ annual appliance sales. This gives them real data, not manufacturer claims or consumer surveys.

### 2. Most Reliable Refrigerator Brands

Refrigerators with fewer features (no dispenser, no smart tech) are more reliable. Among brands, some maintain sub-5% service rates while others exceed 20% in the first year.

### 3. Most Reliable Washer & Dryer Brands

Speed Queen dominates laundry reliability. Their commercial-grade construction translates to fewer breakdowns in residential use. LG's direct-drive motors also show strong reliability.

### 4. Most Reliable Dishwasher Brands

Bosch and Miele lead dishwasher reliability. Their service rates are roughly half the industry average, thanks to simpler mechanical designs and quality components.

### 5. Brands to Be Cautious About

Some well-known brands have surprisingly high repair rates. Yale's data reveals which popular brands underperform on reliability despite strong marketing.

### 6. The Cost of Unreliability

An unreliable appliance doesn't just mean inconvenience — it means service calls ($150-300 each), lost food (refrigerators), and time without a working machine. Reliability should factor into every purchase decision.
