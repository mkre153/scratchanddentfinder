## Where Reviewers Agree

The over-the-range microwave category has one clear trend for 2026: **air fryer combo units are taking over.** Experts across all sources acknowledge that convection/air fry OTR microwaves have gone mainstream, with most major brands now offering them at mid-range prices.

On reliability, LG and GE consistently rank at the top for OTR microwaves. Experts agree that OTR microwaves are fundamentally simple appliances — the main failure points are the magnetron, door latch, and control board, in that order.

## Where Reviewers Disagree

**Air fryer combos — worth it?** Consumer Reports rates air fryer OTR microwaves highly for versatility. Repair-focused experts are more cautious, noting that convection heating elements and additional fans add failure points. The price premium ($100-200 more) may or may not be justified depending on how often you'd actually use the air fry function.

**Ventilation performance:** OTR microwaves double as range hoods, but experts disagree on how well they actually vent. Most OTR units recirculate air rather than venting outside, which limits their effectiveness over gas ranges. Serious cooks may want a dedicated range hood instead. However, for most kitchens, the combination unit saves space and cost.

**Wattage matters:** Some budget experts recommend 700-800 watt models for savings. Cooking experts universally push back — below 1,000 watts, cooking times increase significantly and results are uneven. For S&D buyers, the price difference between 800W and 1,000W models is negligible after the discount.

## S&D Buying Intelligence

OTR microwaves are solid S&D candidates for one key reason: installation hides most damage. Once mounted under your upper cabinets, the top and sides are completely concealed. Only the front door and control panel are visible.

**Best S&D strategy:** Target LG or GE models in the $350-500 retail range. At 30-40% off, you're paying $200-350 for a reliable, brand-name microwave with good ventilation and cooking performance.

**What to avoid:** Any unit with a damaged door latch (safety issue — microwaves won't operate properly with a faulty interlock), a non-functioning turntable motor, or a dented interior cavity that prevents the turntable from rotating.
