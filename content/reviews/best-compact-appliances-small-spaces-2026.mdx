## Where Reviewers Agree

Experts agree that the compact appliance market is driven by one undeniable trend: **urban living demands smaller appliances, and European manufacturers are leading the way.** Bosch and Miele — both European brands with decades of experience building 24-inch appliances for smaller European kitchens — consistently receive top marks in every compact appliance category.

The consensus is also clear that compact appliances have a significant cost premium per unit of capacity. A 24-inch compact dishwasher often costs as much as a full-size 24-inch model. A compact washer-dryer pair can cost more than a full-size set. This is why S&D pricing is especially valuable in this category — the premium pricing creates larger percentage savings.

## Where Reviewers Disagree

**24-inch vs. 18-inch dishwashers:** The standard compact dishwasher is 24 inches wide — the same as full-size models but shorter in height. For truly tight spaces, 18-inch dishwashers exist but experts disagree on whether they're worth buying. Some say the capacity reduction (6-8 place settings vs. 12-14) makes them impractical for anyone beyond a single person. Others argue they're the only option for galley kitchens and the trade-off is necessary.

**All-in-one laundry vs. compact stackables:** This is the central compact laundry debate. *(See our [combo & stackable guide](/reviews/best-washer-dryer-combos-2026))* All-in-one units save the most space but sacrifice washing and drying performance. Compact stackable pairs (24-inch Bosch or Miele) perform much better but require more vertical space. Experts who prioritize performance recommend stackables. Space-limited experts recommend all-in-ones.

**Counter-depth compact refrigerators:** Some experts recommend apartment-size refrigerators (7-12 cubic feet) for small spaces. Others argue that a standard-size counter-depth refrigerator (18-22 cubic feet) fits in most apartment kitchens and is dramatically more practical for daily use. The right answer depends on your actual kitchen layout.

## The Compact Appliance S&D Opportunity

Compact appliances and S&D are a strong combination for several reasons:

**Higher per-unit pricing** means more meaningful S&D savings. A $1,800 Bosch compact washer at 40% off saves $720 — significant for any budget.

**Installation hides damage** even better than full-size. Compact appliances go into tight spaces — between walls, under counters, in closets — where side, top, and back panel damage is invisible.

**Less common at S&D** — compact appliances represent a smaller share of the market, so they appear less frequently at S&D retailers. When you find one, act quickly.

## Category-by-Category Guide

### Compact Dishwashers
- **Top pick:** Bosch 800 Series 24" (ADA-height) — quieter and more reliable than most full-size models
- **S&D tip:** Dishwashers are the safest S&D buy regardless of size — under-counter installation hides everything
- **Avoid:** 18-inch models unless you genuinely have no alternative

### Compact Laundry
- **Top pick:** Bosch 300/500 Series 24" stackable washer + ventless dryer
- **S&D tip:** Verify the stacking kit is included or available separately
- **Key feature:** Ventless heat pump dryer — no external venting needed for apartment installations
- **Avoid:** Generic all-in-one combos from unknown brands — stick with Bosch, Miele, or LG

### Compact Refrigerators
- **Top pick:** Standard counter-depth from LG or Samsung if your kitchen can fit it
- **S&D tip:** Compact fridges have fewer S&D options — be flexible on brand
- **Key consideration:** Measure your space precisely, including door swing clearance

### Compact Ranges
- **Top pick:** 24-inch ranges from GE or Samsung fit standard compact kitchen cutouts
- **S&D tip:** Test every burner — compact ranges have tighter burner spacing and more potential for cross-heating issues
- **Avoid:** Portable or countertop burner setups marketed as "compact ranges"

## Measurement Checklist

Before shopping for compact appliances (S&D or otherwise), measure these:

1. **Width** — to the nearest quarter-inch, including trim or molding
2. **Depth** — from wall to front of counter (for counter-depth), including handles
3. **Height** — from floor to underside of counter or cabinet
4. **Door swing** — how far the door opens in the available space
5. **Electrical** — confirm 120V vs. 240V availability for your space
6. **Plumbing** — water and drain connections for washers and dishwashers
7. **Ventilation** — external vent available, or do you need ventless?
