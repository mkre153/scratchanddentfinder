## Where Reviewers Agree

All expert sources agree on the fundamental trade-off: **stackable separate units (stacked washer + dryer) outperform all-in-one combo units in every performance metric.** Stackables wash better, dry faster, and last longer. If you have space for two stacked units, every expert recommends that over a single combo.

However, experts also agree that **all-in-one combos have improved dramatically** and are a viable option for spaces that genuinely can't accommodate even a compact stackable pair. LG's ventless combo units, in particular, receive consistent praise for reliability improvements.

## Where Reviewers Disagree

**Ventless vs. vented dryers:** For stackable pairs in apartments, ventless heat pump dryers are the growing recommendation. They don't need external venting, which opens up placement options. However, they take 20-40% longer to dry clothes. Consumer Reports says the time trade-off is worth it for the installation flexibility. Repair-focused experts note that heat pump dryers have more complex internals.

**Combo unit viability:** Some experts flatly recommend against all-in-one combos due to long cycle times (3-5 hours for wash+dry) and higher failure rates. Others acknowledge that for single-person or couple households doing 2-3 loads per week, modern combo units are perfectly adequate. The truth depends entirely on your laundry volume.

**Brand loyalty vs. best-in-class:** Should you buy a matched washer-dryer set from one brand, or pick the best washer and best dryer separately? Experts split on this. Matching sets from LG, Samsung, and GE offer aesthetic consistency and sometimes stacking kit compatibility. But mixing brands (e.g., an LG washer with a Bosch dryer) can get you the best of each category.

## The Small-Space Equation

The compact laundry market breaks down into three categories:

**Full-size stackables (27" width):**
- Standard capacity (4.5-5.0 cu ft wash, 7.0-8.0 cu ft dry)
- Require stacking kit ($30-80)
- Best performance of all space-saving options
- S&D availability: moderate

**Compact stackables (24" width):**
- Smaller capacity (2.2-2.5 cu ft wash, 4.0-4.5 cu ft dry)
- European sizing, fits in closets
- Bosch and Miele dominate this segment
- S&D availability: limited

**All-in-one combos:**
- Single unit does both washing and drying
- Smallest footprint but longest cycle times
- LG leads with ventless technology
- S&D availability: limited

## S&D Buying Intelligence

Washer-dryer combos and stackables are premium-priced, which means S&D savings can be substantial — $400-1,000 per unit. But they also require more careful inspection:

**Why they're riskier as S&D:**
- More complex internals (especially combo units and heat pump dryers)
- Drainage and pump systems can sustain hidden shipping damage
- Electronic control boards manage multiple functions and are sensitive to impacts

**What makes them worth it as S&D:**
- Installation typically hides side and back damage (closet, between walls)
- Premium pricing means S&D discounts of 35-50% are common
- A matched stackable pair at S&D can save you $800-1,500 vs. retail

**Inspection priority:** Run a complete wash cycle if the retailer allows it. Check for water leaks, unusual vibration during spin, and verify the drain pump engages. For stackables, confirm the stacking kit is included or separately available.
