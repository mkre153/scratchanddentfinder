## The 10-Minute Inspection That Saves Hundreds

Buying scratch and dent appliances is one of the smartest ways to save on major purchases — but only if you know what to inspect. Most cosmetic damage is irrelevant once installed, but functional issues can turn a great deal into an expensive mistake.

This guide covers every appliance category with specific inspection checklists. Spend 10 minutes inspecting at the store and you'll buy with confidence.

## Universal Rules (All Appliances)

Before diving into category-specific checklists, here are the rules that apply to every S&D purchase:

1. **Power it on.** Any reputable retailer will let you test the unit. If they refuse, leave.
2. **Check the model number.** Search it on your phone. Is it current? Is it recalled? Is it discontinued?
3. **Understand what gets hidden.** Side, back, and top panel dents disappear after installation in most kitchens.
4. **Ask about the warranty.** Most S&D units carry full manufacturer warranty. Verify before buying.
5. **Know the return policy.** Get it in writing. Reputable stores offer 30-day minimum returns.

## Refrigerator Inspection Checklist

Refrigerators are the most common S&D appliance and one of the smartest buys. *(See our [refrigerator guide](/reviews/best-refrigerators-2026))*

**Must test:**
- Compressor running — listen for quiet, steady hum (clicking or rattling = walk away)
- Ice maker cycle (if equipped) — the #1 failure point
- Water dispenser flow and filter housing integrity
- Both doors seal firmly with no gaps
- Interior lights activate when doors open
- Temperature reaches 37°F (fridge) and 0°F (freezer) within a reasonable time

**Damage that's OK:**
- Side panel dents (hidden between cabinets)
- Top dents (hidden under cabinets)
- Back panel dents (hidden against wall)
- Minor stainless steel scratches (buff out with stainless cleaner)

**Deal-breakers:**
- Compressor noise (clicking, rattling, grinding)
- Door seal gaps (causes frost, increases energy cost)
- Cracked shelving or door bins (replacement cost adds up)
- Non-functioning ice maker (expensive repair, $200-400)

## Washer & Dryer Inspection Checklist

Laundry appliances are excellent S&D candidates. *(See our [washer & dryer guide](/reviews/best-washers-dryers-2026))*

**Must test:**
- Run a quick wash cycle — listen for unusual vibration during spin
- Spin the dryer drum by hand — should rotate smoothly
- Check all hose connections for signs of leaking
- Verify the control panel and all cycle selections respond
- Test the door latch — should close firmly

**Damage that's OK:**
- Side panel dents (hidden when side-by-side or stacked)
- Top panel dents on dryers (hidden in closet setups)
- Back panel scratches and dents (against wall)
- Minor finish scratches on stainless or white

**Deal-breakers:**
- Drum grinding noise (worn bearings, $300-500 repair)
- Water leaking from any connection or the pump area
- Drum not spinning (motor or belt issue)
- Burning smell during operation (electrical problem)

## Dishwasher Inspection Checklist

Dishwashers may be the single best S&D appliance category. *(See our [dishwasher guide](/reviews/best-dishwashers-2026))*

**Must test:**
- Open the door and check spray arms rotate freely
- Inspect the door gasket for tears or gaps
- Slide all racks in and out — bent rails are expensive to fix
- Verify the control panel responds to all buttons
- Look at the interior tub for rust spots

**Damage that's OK:**
- Top, side, and back panel dents (completely hidden under counter)
- Minor door panel scratches on stainless (can be buffed)
- Packaging damage with no visible dents (best-case scenario)

**Deal-breakers:**
- Bent or broken rack rails (replacement racks cost $80-150)
- Door gasket tears (causes leaking)
- Interior tub rust (indicates moisture damage)
- Non-responsive control panel

## Range & Oven Inspection Checklist

Ranges offer strong S&D value. *(See our [range guide](/reviews/best-ranges-ovens-2026))*

**Must test:**
- Every burner — gas should have even flame, electric should heat evenly
- Oven at 350°F — should reach temperature within 15 minutes
- Oven door opens, closes, and seals properly
- Broiler function if equipped
- Convection fan runs without noise

**Damage that's OK:**
- Side panel dents (hidden between cabinets)
- Back panel dents (against wall)
- Minor scratches on stainless front

**Deal-breakers:**
- Glass cooktop cracks (safety hazard, not just cosmetic)
- Bent oven door hinges (won't seal, causes heat loss)
- Gas burners with uneven or yellow flame (gas valve issue)
- Non-functioning control panel or oven thermostat

## The Final Check

Before you pay, run through this quick summary:

1. Did you power it on and test every feature? **Yes / No**
2. Is the damage cosmetic only, in areas hidden after installation? **Yes / No**
3. Does it carry the full manufacturer warranty? **Yes / No**
4. Does the retailer offer a return policy (30+ days)? **Yes / No**
5. Is it a reliable brand? *(Check our [reliability guide](/reviews/most-reliable-appliance-brands-2026))* **Yes / No**

Five yeses = confident purchase. Any no = proceed with caution or walk away.
