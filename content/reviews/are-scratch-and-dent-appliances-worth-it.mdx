## What Are Scratch & Dent Appliances?

Let's clear up the biggest misconception first: **scratch and dent appliances are brand-new, never-used units.** They are not refurbished. They are not returned products. They are not floor models (though some retailers mix these in — always ask).

Scratch and dent appliances are brand-new units that sustained cosmetic damage during manufacturing, shipping, or warehouse handling. A forklift nicks a side panel. A refrigerator gets a dent during truck delivery. A dishwasher's packaging gets crushed but the unit inside is perfect. These units can't be sold as "new" at retail, so manufacturers and retailers sell them at significant discounts.

## The Real Savings

Based on data from thousands of S&D retailers across the US, here's what typical savings look like:

- **Packaging damage only (no visible dents):** 20-30% off retail
- **Minor dents on sides or back:** 30-50% off retail
- **Visible front panel damage:** 40-60% off retail
- **Multiple cosmetic issues:** 50-70% off retail

On a $2,000 refrigerator, that's $600-1,400 in savings. On a $1,200 washer, you're saving $360-840. These are real, brand-new appliances with real discounts.

## Where the Savings Make the Most Sense

Not all appliances are equally smart as S&D purchases. The key is understanding what damage gets hidden after installation:

**Best S&D categories:**
- **Dishwashers** — Only the front door panel shows after under-counter installation. Sides, top, and back are completely hidden. *(See our [dishwasher guide](/reviews/best-dishwashers-2026))*
- **Ranges** — Install between cabinets, hiding side panel damage. Back is against the wall. *(See our [range guide](/reviews/best-ranges-ovens-2026))*
- **Refrigerators** — Side panels often hidden by cabinets. Counter-depth models especially. *(See our [refrigerator guide](/reviews/best-refrigerators-2026))*
- **Freezers** — Usually in garages or basements where cosmetics don't matter at all. *(See our [freezer guide](/reviews/best-freezers-2026))*

**More challenging S&D categories:**
- **Washers and dryers** — Often in visible laundry rooms, but side-by-side installation hides side dents
- **Microwaves** — Smaller savings in absolute dollars but OTR models hide top/side damage

## The Warranty Question

This is the #1 concern for S&D buyers, and the answer is reassuring: **most scratch and dent appliances carry the full manufacturer warranty.** The cosmetic damage doesn't void the warranty on internal components.

However, there are important nuances:

- **Always verify** — Ask the retailer to confirm the warranty status before purchasing. Some heavily discounted units may have modified warranty terms.
- **Register the unit** — Register your appliance with the manufacturer just like you would a full-price purchase.
- **Keep your receipt** — The S&D retailer receipt is your proof of purchase for warranty claims.
- **Cosmetic damage isn't covered** — The existing dents and scratches you bought the unit with are obviously not covered. But compressor failures, motor issues, and other mechanical problems are.

## What to Inspect Before Buying

Smart S&D shopping requires a quick but thorough inspection:

1. **Power it on** — Any reputable S&D retailer will let you test the unit. If they won't, walk away.
2. **Check the model number** — Verify it's a current or recent model, not discontinued.
3. **Assess the damage honestly** — Will the dents be visible after installation? Side dents on appliances that go between cabinets don't matter.
4. **Test every feature** — Run the ice maker, test the burners, spin the dryer drum, cycle the dishwasher.
5. **Ask about the return policy** — Reputable retailers offer at least 30-day returns.

## The Bottom Line

Are scratch and dent appliances worth it? **For informed buyers, absolutely.** You're getting brand-new, warrantied appliances from major manufacturers at 30-70% off. The "damage" that justifies the discount often becomes invisible once the appliance is installed in your kitchen.
