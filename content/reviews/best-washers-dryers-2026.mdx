## Full Outline

### 1. Front-Load vs Top-Load

Front-load washers consistently outperform top-load in cleaning, water efficiency, and spin speed. The trade-off is a higher purchase price and the need to leave the door open between loads to prevent mold.

### 2. Best Budget Washer

For budget-conscious buyers, look for a washer with at least 4.5 cubic feet capacity and a direct-drive motor. Avoid models that skimp on the drum material — stainless steel drums last longer.

### 3. Best Overall Washer

The best overall washers balance cleaning performance, noise levels, and long-term reliability. Yale's service data reveals which brands actually hold up after year one.

### 4. Best Dryer

Modern dryers offer steam refresh cycles and sensor drying that prevents over-drying. Heat pump dryers are the most energy-efficient but add 20-30 minutes to cycle times.

### 5. Most Reliable Brands

Speed Queen dominates laundry reliability with the lowest service rates in Yale's database. LG and Bosch also perform well, while some popular brands have surprisingly high repair rates.
