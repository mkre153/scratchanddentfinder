Dishwashers are the best-kept secret in scratch and dent appliance shopping.

The reason is simple: a dishwasher sits inside a cabinet opening with its sides, top, and back permanently hidden from view. The only visible surface is the front door face — and many dishwashers accept a cabinet panel that hides even that.

When you buy a scratch and dent dishwasher with a dented side panel, you are buying a cosmetic flaw that will be invisible from the moment the appliance is installed. You're saving 20–35% for a flaw you'll never see.

## Why Dishwashers Are the #1 Scratch and Dent Buy

**Permanent concealment.** The installation cavity hides every surface except the front door panel. Side dents, top dings, and back damage are invisible immediately after install.

**Panel-ready models take this further.** Panel-ready dishwashers accept a custom cabinet door panel, making the entire visible surface match your cabinetry. The cosmetic damage is triple-hidden — behind the panel, inside the cabinet opening, and against the adjacent walls.

**Premium brands are available.** Bosch is the most commonly available premium brand at scratch and dent outlets. Bosch dishwashers retail for $800–$1,200 and appear at outlets for $450–$750. The washing performance is identical.

## What to Inspect

Despite being the safest scratch and dent buy, a few checks are essential:

### 1. Door Latch
Close the door firmly and verify it latches securely. The dishwasher won't run with an improperly latched door — a damaged latch is a functional issue, not cosmetic.

### 2. Door Seal
Inspect the rubber seal around the door perimeter. Look for tears or sections that have separated from the door frame. A damaged seal will leak during operation.

### 3. Water Inlet Valve
The water inlet valve is located at the bottom rear of the dishwasher. Inspect it for cracks. A cracked inlet valve will leak when connected — this is a $15–30 part but worth knowing before purchase.

### 4. Spray Arms
Open the door and check that both spray arms (upper and lower) are present and spin freely. Missing or cracked spray arms affect wash coverage.

### 5. Interior Tub
Look for dents inside the tub. Minor interior dents are typically harmless, but large dents that protrude into the interior space can interfere with rack operation.

### 6. Control Panel
Test the controls if the unit is plugged in. Scratched controls are fine; non-responsive buttons indicate damage beyond cosmetic.

**Standard dishwashers** (stainless steel or black/white front panel) are more commonly available at scratch and dent outlets and typically carry lower discounts. The front panel is the only visible surface, so front scratches are slightly more noticeable.
