All that damage that knocked 20-40% off the price? Invisible.

### Why Dishwashers Are the Perfect Scratch and Dent Buy

Every appliance has some surfaces hidden after installation. Refrigerators hide the sides and back. Ranges hide the sides. But dishwashers hide nearly everything.

<Callout type="savings">
Typical savings: $100-$500 off retail. A $900 mid-range dishwasher with a dented top panel might be $550-$650 — and you'll never see that dent again after installation.
</Callout>

Here's what's hidden once a dishwasher is installed:

- **Top panel** — completely under the countertop
- **Both side panels** — pressed against cabinet walls
- **Back panel** — against the wall, behind plumbing connections
- **Bottom and feet** — behind the kick plate

The only visible surface is the door front. And if you opt for a panel-ready model that accepts a custom cabinet panel, even that is hidden.

### The Door Latch: Your Most Important Check

If there's one thing that separates a great scratch and dent dishwasher deal from a bad one, it's the door latch.

Dishwashers fill with water during every cycle. The door latch and gasket are what keep that water inside the tub instead of on your kitchen floor. If the dishwasher took an impact near the door during shipping, the latch mechanism or door alignment could be slightly off.

<Callout type="warning">
Close the door and check for these problems: the door doesn't click firmly, you can see light around the gasket edge, the door sits at a slight angle when closed, or you need to push extra hard to get it to latch. Any of these issues means potential leaks. Walk away or negotiate a steep discount that covers the cost of repair.
</Callout>

A failed door latch isn't just inconvenient — water leaking from a dishwasher can damage kitchen flooring, subfloor, and the cabinets on either side. This is the one functional issue that's worth walking away over.

### Racks and Spray Arms: Daily Usability

After the door, racks and spray arms are the components that most affect your day-to-day experience.

**Racks** should slide smoothly in and out on their rollers. If a rack was jarred during shipping and the rollers popped off their tracks, it's usually an easy fix — but ask the store to fix it before you buy, or negotiate a further discount.

Check all the tines (the fold-up prongs that hold plates and cups). A few bent tines are cosmetic and easily bent back into place. A rack with multiple broken tines may need replacement, which costs $50-$100 depending on the model.

**Spray arms** should spin freely when you push them with your hand. A bent spray arm won't distribute water evenly, leaving dishes dirty. Replacement spray arms are available for $15-$40, but it's better to find a unit where they're intact.

### Will It Fit? Sizing Concerns Addressed

Standard built-in dishwashers are 24 inches wide, 24 inches deep, and approximately 34 inches tall. Your cabinet opening was built to accommodate these dimensions with a small amount of clearance.

Minor exterior dents don't change the overall dimensions enough to cause fit problems. A dent pushes the metal inward, not outward — so it actually takes up less space, not more.

The only scenario where fit becomes an issue is if the side panel has a severe structural bulge outward (from being hit on the opposite side). This is extremely rare on dishwashers. If you're concerned, bring your tape measure and check the width at the widest point of the dented area.

### Installation Considerations

Scratch and dent dishwashers install identically to retail units. The mounting brackets, water supply connection, drain hose, and electrical connection are all in standard locations that aren't affected by cosmetic panel damage.

One thing to verify: check that the mounting bracket holes on the top of the unit align properly. In rare cases, a dent on the top panel can slightly shift the bracket position. This is easily corrected with a washer or by drilling a new pilot hole, but it's worth noting during inspection.
