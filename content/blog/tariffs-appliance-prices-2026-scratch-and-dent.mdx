Appliance prices in 2026 are moving in one direction: up. The 10% baseline tariff on all U.S. imports - in place through at least July 2026, with a threatened 15% increase pending - is squeezing manufacturers and retailers alike. For consumers buying new, the math is getting harder to ignore. For scratch-and-dent shoppers, the math is actually getting better.

Here's why tariffs create a window of opportunity in the S&D market - and how to take advantage of it before that window closes.

## Why Tariffs Hit Appliances Hard

Major appliances aren't made from purely domestic parts. A washing machine assembled in Ohio still contains motors, control boards, and electronic components sourced from China, Vietnam, or Mexico. A refrigerator built in South Carolina still runs on a compressor manufactured in Monterrey.

The tariff structure amplifies costs at multiple points:

**Finished goods imports.** Some appliance categories are heavily import-dependent. Many dishwashers sold under American brands are manufactured in Mexico or Poland. Tariffs on Mexican goods, combined with steel and aluminum tariffs, can add $100-$250 to the cost of a mid-range unit before it reaches the retailer.

**Component sourcing.** Even domestically assembled appliances depend on imported parts. The 145% tariff on Chinese goods - the highest in the current structure - directly impacts motors, inverters, and smart home electronics that are predominantly Chinese-sourced.

**Supply chain uncertainty.** Manufacturers can't easily switch suppliers overnight. A new supplier relationship for a critical component takes 12-18 months to establish. In the meantime, existing suppliers pass tariff costs downstream.

The Yale Budget Lab estimates that under the current tariff schedule, the average American household will pay an additional $3,800 per year on consumer goods. Appliances are among the hardest-hit categories.

## The S&D Advantage in a Tariff Environment

Scratch-and-dent pricing works off a different baseline than new retail pricing. Here's the key dynamic:

S&D dealers - whether big-box outlet stores or independent warehouse dealers - price their inventory based on what *they paid* for it, not what a replacement unit costs today. A dealer who acquired a French door refrigerator six months ago at $900 wholesale prices it at $1,100-$1,300 in their showroom, regardless of what that model costs to manufacture or import today.

That means pre-tariff S&D inventory carries pre-tariff pricing. The units in S&D warehouses right now were manufactured and shipped before the tariff increases hit. They were priced before dealers factored in new cost structures.

<Callout type="tip">
Once dealers sell through their current S&D stock and restock at post-tariff costs, S&D prices will rise to match. The window for pre-tariff S&D deals is real but finite. April-June 2026 is the prime window.
</Callout>

## Which Appliance Categories Feel the Most Tariff Pressure?

**Refrigerators.** Compressors, inverter boards, and smart home electronics in modern refrigerators are heavily China-sourced. The 145% tariff on Chinese goods hits refrigerator manufacturing costs harder than almost any other appliance category. Consumer Reports has flagged refrigerators as one of the top categories to buy before tariff pricing fully flows through to shelves.

**Dishwashers.** Bosch, Miele, and several American brand dishwashers are assembled in Germany, Poland, or Mexico - all markets subject to the 10% baseline tariff or higher. Major brands like Whirlpool assemble some dishwasher lines in Mexico, which faces additional tariff pressure. Expect 12-18% price increases on new dishwashers by summer 2026.

**Laundry.** Front-load washers and dryers rely on motors and electronic control systems with significant Asian component sourcing. LG and Samsung, both Korean brands with Chinese-component supply chains, face the steepest cost increases. Domestic brands like Speed Queen have more insulation but still source electronics internationally.

**Cooking Ranges.** Ranges have more domestic manufacturing than other categories, which provides some insulation. However, igniters, control boards, and burner components still come from overseas. Expect modest 8-12% increases on new ranges - less dramatic than refrigerators or dishwashers, but still material.

<Callout type="tip">
If you're buying one appliance, prioritize the refrigerator. It faces the steepest tariff pressure and represents the highest dollar savings in the S&D market right now.
</Callout>

## The Supply Squeeze Beyond Tariffs

Tariff costs are the headline, but supply chain disruption is the secondary effect that may matter more by Q3 2026.

Analysis from UVA Darden School of Business on trade disruption patterns suggests that sudden tariff implementation creates ordering volatility: manufacturers pull forward orders to get ahead of tariffs, then face inventory gluts, then experience shortages when supply chain partners adjust to new cost structures. For consumers, this translates to extended lead times on new appliance orders - 6-12 weeks for in-demand models versus the usual 2-3 weeks.

S&D inventory bypasses this entirely. The unit is on the floor. You can take it home today or schedule delivery for next week. No back-order queue, no waitlist, no "check back in 8 weeks."

For landlords with a unit sitting empty or flippers on a tight renovation timeline, S&D availability isn't just a cost advantage - it's an operational one.

## What to Buy Now vs. What Can Wait

**Buy now:** Refrigerators, dishwashers, washer/dryer sets. These face the highest tariff exposure and the most supply chain volatility. Pre-tariff S&D inventory is available now at prices that won't persist once dealers restock.

**Can wait (somewhat):** Cooking ranges. Less tariff exposure means less urgency, though prices will still rise. If you're flexible, you have a slightly longer window here.

**Wait on:** Small appliances (microwaves, range hoods). These categories have more pricing elasticity and less S&D inventory turnover pressure. No burning reason to rush.

The clearest calculus is this: if you need a refrigerator or dishwasher and you were already considering S&D, the tariff environment makes that decision easier. The savings are larger and more durable than they've been in recent years.

## How to Find S&D Inventory Now

**Big-box outlet stores.** Home Depot Outlet, Lowe's Outlet, and Best Buy Outlet locations carry scratch-and-dent, open-box, and clearance appliances. Inventory rotates quickly - checking weekly is worth it for major purchases.

**Independent S&D dealers.** Local warehouse dealers often have deeper inventory and more negotiating flexibility than big-box outlets. Prices aren't always posted; asking for a discount on items that have been on the floor for more than 30 days often works.

**ScratchAndDentFinder.com.** Aggregates S&D inventory from dealers nationwide. Filter by appliance category, brand, and location. Useful for getting a sense of regional market pricing before you visit a dealer.

<Callout type="tip">
When visiting a dealer, ask specifically: "What do you have that's been on the floor longest?" Dealers want to move aging inventory and often have the most flexibility on price for units that have been sitting 60+ days.
</Callout>

When evaluating any S&D unit, the standard inspection checklist applies: check for functional damage (not just cosmetic), test all settings if possible, confirm warranty or return terms, and verify the serial number against CPSC recall databases. Tariff urgency is real, but don't let it rush you past the due diligence that protects you from buying a problem appliance.

## Bottom Line

Tariffs are making new appliances more expensive, and that trend isn't reversing in 2026. The 10% baseline tariff on all imports - layered on top of 145% tariffs on Chinese goods and threatened increases - is flowing through to retail prices on a 60-90 day lag.

S&D inventory priced before those increases represents real value that compounds on top of the existing 30-50% S&D discount. Refrigerators, dishwashers, and laundry units face the steepest tariff pressure and the best savings opportunity in the S&D market right now.

The window is finite. Pre-tariff inventory won't last indefinitely, and once dealers restock at post-tariff costs, S&D prices will adjust upward to match. If you're in the market for a major appliance, April 2026 is a genuinely good time to buy - and the S&D channel is the place to do it.
