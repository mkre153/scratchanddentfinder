Laundry appliances are the second-best scratch and dent category after refrigerators. Washers and dryers are large, easily damaged in transit, sold in high volumes, and the cosmetic damage is usually on the front panel or top — areas that face the wall or are at eye level but don't affect function.

A front-load washer/dryer set retailing at $2,000+ regularly appears at scratch and dent outlets for $900–$1,400. The savings on a set can be $600–$1,000.

## What Makes Laundry Appliances Good Scratch and Dent Buys

**Damage is usually cosmetic.** Dents on the top, sides, or front panel do not affect wash or dry performance. Control panel scratches are cosmetic unless the controls are damaged.

**Sets generate extra negotiating power.** Retailers who receive damaged washer/dryer sets want to move both units together. Offering to buy the set often unlocks 5–10% additional discount.

**High volume = high availability.** Laundry appliances are sold at enormous volume. More units moving through warehouses means more cosmetic damage and more inventory at scratch and dent outlets.

## Front-Load vs. Top-Load: Which is Better?

| Type | Savings | Pros | Scratch & Dent Risk |

| Front-Load Washer | 25–40% | Energy efficient, stackable | Door seal damage — inspect carefully |
| Top-Load Washer | 20–35% | Simpler mechanism | Lid damage is common but harmless |
| Front-Load Dryer | 25–40% | Stackable, energy efficient | Door hinge damage — test before buying |
| Top-Load Dryer | 20–30% | Lower price, widely available | Lint trap damage — cheap to fix |

**Front-load units** offer better energy efficiency and are stackable, but require closer inspection of the door seal. A torn door seal on a front-load washer will leak — it's a $20–60 repair but worth knowing before you buy.

**Top-load units** are simpler mechanically and easier to verify. Lid damage is the most common cosmetic issue and is entirely harmless.

## What to Inspect Before You Buy

### Washer Checks

**1. Water inlet connections** — Inspect the hot and cold water inlet ports on the back for cracks or damage. A cracked port will leak when connected.

**2. Door seal (front-load)** — Run your finger around the entire door seal. Look for tears, holes, or sections that have separated from the drum housing. Small nicks are acceptable; tears that go through the seal are not.

**3. Drum** — Spin the drum manually. It should rotate smoothly without scraping or catching. Resistance suggests a bearing issue unrelated to cosmetic damage.

**4. Control panel** — Test all controls if the unit is plugged in. Control panel scratches are cosmetic; non-responsive buttons indicate damage beyond cosmetic.

**5. Test cycle** — Ask the retailer to run a rinse and spin cycle. This confirms water inlet, drum rotation, and drain function in under 10 minutes.

### Dryer Checks

**1. Exhaust port** — The rear exhaust port must be intact. A damaged port prevents proper venting, which is a fire hazard. This is a deal-breaker.

**2. Drum rotation** — Turn the drum manually. Smooth rotation is expected; scraping or rattling suggests interior damage.

**3. Door and latch** — Confirm the door seals completely and the latch engages. A door that doesn't seal reduces dryer efficiency.

**4. Heating element (electric)** — If the unit is plugged in, run it for 60 seconds and hold your hand at the drum opening. You should feel heat within the first minute.

**5. Gas line fitting (gas dryers)** — Inspect the gas fitting for damage. If it looks bent or damaged, pass on the unit.

## Buying a Set vs. Individual Units

If both washer and dryer are available at the outlet, buy the set. Here's why:

- **Bundle discount**: Retailers almost always negotiate on sets. The goal is to move both units — offer to buy both and ask for 10–15% off the combined price.
- **Matching controls**: Same-brand matched units often have synchronized controls and matching aesthetics.
- **Stacking compatibility**: Matching front-load sets are stackable with an inexpensive stacking kit.

If only one unit is available, buying just a washer or dryer separately is still a strong deal.

## The Bottom Line

Laundry appliances are an excellent scratch and dent buy. Front-load units carry slightly more inspection risk (door seal), but the savings justify the extra 10 minutes of inspection.

Buy the set when you can. Inspect the door seal on front-loaders. Run a test cycle before you leave the lot.

**Is it safe to buy a scratch and dent washing machine?** Yes. Cosmetic damage does not affect wash performance. Check water inlet connections, door seal integrity (front-load), and run a test cycle.

**What should I check on a scratch and dent washer?** Water inlet connections for cracks, the door seal on front-load units for tears, confirm the drum spins freely, and ask the retailer to run a short cycle.

**Do scratch and dent dryers have issues?** Dryers are the safest laundry buy. The critical check is venting — confirm the exhaust port is undamaged and the heating element works.

**Should I buy a matching set?** If both are available, yes. Matching sets are easier to negotiate as a bundle, and retailers are more motivated to move both units together.

**Can I stack a scratch and dent washer and dryer?** Yes. Most front-load units can be stacked with a stacking kit ($20–60). Verify the models are stackable before purchasing.
