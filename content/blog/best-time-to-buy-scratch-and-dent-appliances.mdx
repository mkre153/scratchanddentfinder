Scratch and dent appliances offer savings of 20–60% off retail year-round, but timing your purchase strategically can squeeze out an extra 10–20% beyond the base discount. Retailers cycle inventory based on model-year transitions, major sale events, and seasonal demand — and knowing how those cycles work puts you in the best position.

This guide breaks down the best months to shop, explains why certain times of year flood the market with inventory, and gives you a month-by-month playbook.

## Why Timing Matters for Scratch and Dent

Most scratch and dent outlets source inventory from three places:

1. **Major retailers** offloading floor models and damaged warehouse stock
2. **Manufacturer overruns** and returned appliances from big-box stores
3. **Customer returns** after failed deliveries or cosmetic refusals

The volume of available inventory tracks closely with retail sales cycles. When big-box stores push heavy appliance promotions, more units move through warehouses, more get damaged in transit, and more end up at scratch and dent outlets 2–4 weeks later.

## Month-by-Month Breakdown

### January — Best Month Overall

January is the strongest month for scratch and dent deals. Three forces converge:

- Retailers clear holiday overstock to make room for new model-year inventory
- Black Friday and holiday trade-ins flood the secondary market
- Post-holiday slow consumer demand means prices drop to move units

**What to look for:** Refrigerators, dishwashers, and laundry units from the prior year's model run. These often carry the same specs as the current model with deeper discounts.

**Action:** Check your local outlets in the first two weeks of January. Inventory is highest before it gets picked over.

### February — Still Strong

Momentum from January carries through February. Retailers continue clearing year-end inventory and new model-year stock hasn't fully arrived yet.

**What to look for:** Full appliance suites for kitchen or laundry rooms. Buying multiple pieces from the same brand at once often unlocks better negotiating leverage.

### March — Inventory Transitions

March marks the start of spring buying season. New model-year appliances begin hitting retail shelves, pushing older inventory to scratch and dent channels.

**What to look for:** Range/oven deals. Spring often brings strong range inventory as kitchen remodels pick up and homeowners upgrade.

### April — Spring Sweet Spot

Home improvement season accelerates in April. Moving activity picks up, which increases appliance turnover and sends more units through scratch and dent channels.

**Best for:** Dishwashers and refrigerators. These are the most frequently replaced appliances during home sales and renovations.

**Pro tip:** [Browse scratch and dent dishwashers on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=scratch+and+dent+dishwasher&tag=scratchanddentfinder-20) — April often has excellent availability.

### May — Memorial Day Surge

Memorial Day weekend is one of the biggest appliance sale events of the year. This creates a ripple effect: the surge in new appliance sales generates a corresponding wave of floor models, open-box units, and damaged stock flowing into scratch and dent channels in June.

**What to do in May:** Scout inventory now. Prices may be higher right before Memorial Day as outlets hold stock in anticipation of demand. If you can wait until June, do.

### June — Post-Memorial Day Payoff

The wave of Memorial Day trade-ins and overstock hits scratch and dent stores in June. This is one of the highest-volume inventory months of the year.

**What to look for:** Washers and dryers. Laundry appliances see heavy Memorial Day sales and generate strong secondary inventory.

**Shop now:** [Scratch and dent washing machines on Amazon](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=scratch+and+dent+washing+machine&tag=scratchanddentfinder-20)

### July — Fourth of July Deals

Independence Day generates another appliance sales event at major retailers, similar to Memorial Day. Scratch and dent outlets often run coordinated promotions during the holiday weekend.

**Action:** Check local store promotions the week of July 4th. Many outlets discount floor models 10–15% extra during holiday weekends to drive traffic.

### August — Back-to-School Transitions

August is quieter for appliance deals, but it's the last month before September's model-year transition. If you're flexible on exact model, August can offer good selection before September clears the shelves.

**Best for:** Compact appliances and apartment-sized units, which often spike in availability as back-to-school moves drive apartment turnovers.

### September — Second-Best Month

September rivals January as the best month to shop. New appliance model years officially launch, and retailers aggressively discount all prior-year inventory. Scratch and dent outlets benefit directly from this cycle.

**What to look for:** Premium brands (Samsung, LG, Bosch) running prior-year models with the steepest discounts. The specs are nearly identical to the new model at a fraction of the price.

**Pro tip:** [Shop scratch and dent refrigerators in September](https://www.amazon.com/s?k=scratch+and+dent+refrigerator&tag=scratchanddentfinder-20) — French door and counter-depth models see heavy inventory at model-year changeover.

### October — Solid Value

October maintains good inventory from September's model-year flush. Selection is slightly lower as units get picked over through the month, but deals remain strong.

**Action:** If you missed September, shop early October. The best units from the model-year transition disappear quickly.

### November — Pre-Black Friday Caution

Scratch and dent outlets often raise prices slightly in early November as they anticipate Black Friday traffic. Avoid paying a premium by shopping at the very start of November or waiting until late November.

**What actually happens at Black Friday:** Big-box Black Friday deals drive volume, but scratch and dent outlets rarely discount as aggressively. The real payoff from Black Friday comes in December and January.

### December — Holiday Inventory Build

December is mixed. Holiday gifting creates some new inventory flow, but demand from gift-buyers keeps prices elevated at most outlets.

**Best strategy for December:** Use the time to scout inventory and build relationships with local retailers. Ask them when post-holiday inventory typically arrives (usually early January) and whether they can hold a unit.

## The Inventory Rhythm: What to Do Every Week

Scratch and dent inventory turns over fast. The best strategy isn't about hitting the perfect month — it's about showing up consistently.

1. **Call ahead**: Ask when the next delivery arrives and show up the same day
2. **Build a relationship**: Repeat customers at local outlets often get first call on new stock
3. **Move fast**: Good units sell within days. If the price works, buy it
4. **Negotiate**: Prices on floor models and slow-moving inventory are almost always negotiable, especially at independent outlets

**What month has the best scratch and dent deals?** January and September are historically the strongest months. January clears holiday overstock, and September marks the start of new appliance model years.

**Is Black Friday good for scratch and dent?** Yes, but indirectly. The best scratch and dent inventory often appears in the weeks after Black Friday, not during it.

**Do scratch and dent stores have seasonal sales?** Many do. Look for Memorial Day, Labor Day, and Fourth of July promotions.

**How often does inventory turn over?** Most stores receive new inventory weekly or bi-weekly. Calling ahead to ask when the next delivery arrives is the best strategy.

**Should I wait for a sale?** If you see the appliance you need at a good price, buy it. Waiting risks losing the unit to another buyer.
