Refrigerators are the single best appliance category to buy scratch and dent. The reasons are straightforward: cosmetic damage happens almost exclusively on large flat panels and door edges, the damage has zero effect on function, and the discount is largest on the most expensive models.

A French door refrigerator with a door dent retails for $2,800 new. The same unit with a 3-inch door dent goes for $1,400–$1,900 at a scratch and dent outlet. The ice maker works. The cooling works. The dent is on the side facing your cabinets.

## What Makes Refrigerators the Best Scratch and Dent Buy

**Damage is usually hidden.** Most refrigerators are installed with their sides against cabinets or walls. A dent on a side panel is permanently invisible once the unit is in place.

**The functional risk is low.** Unlike washers or dishwashers where water systems can fail, refrigerators have simple, reliable cooling systems. Cosmetic damage rarely correlates with functional problems.

**Savings are proportional to price.** Larger, more expensive refrigerators (French door, counter-depth, professional) command the steepest percentage discounts at scratch and dent outlets because the cosmetic damage is more noticeable on their large panels.

## What to Inspect Before You Buy

### 1. Door Seals
Run your hand around all door seals. The seal should be firmly attached with no tears, gaps, or deformation. A compromised seal means the refrigerator works harder to maintain temperature — higher energy bills and faster compressor wear.

### 2. Cooling Function
Ask the retailer to confirm the unit is plugged in and cooling. If possible, open the fridge and feel for cold air, or bring a small thermometer and check the internal temperature. A refrigerator should maintain 35–38°F.

### 3. Water and Ice Dispenser Lines
Units with dispenser damage are worth avoiding unless the discount is steep and you're comfortable with a repair. Check the dispenser for cracks, test the water line if connected, and inspect the ice maker for ice buildup that might indicate a prior leak.

### 4. Interior Shelving and Drawers
Missing or cracked shelves and broken vegetable drawers are common on scratch and dent units. Minor damage is fine, but a missing shelf that's no longer manufactured can be a problem. Check availability before committing.

### 5. Leveling Feet
Refrigerators that were tipped or dropped during transport may have bent leveling feet. Test that all four feet contact the floor evenly — a poorly leveled refrigerator can cause compressor noise and door alignment issues.

## The Bottom Line

A scratch and dent refrigerator is one of the best financial decisions you can make when buying appliances. The savings are real, the functional risk is minimal, and the most common type of damage is either invisible after installation or easily ignored.

Focus on door seals and cooling function. Everything else is cosmetic.

**Are scratch and dent refrigerators reliable?** Yes. Cosmetic damage does not affect performance or reliability. The compressor, cooling system, and electronics are unaffected by surface damage.

**What should I inspect?** Check door seals for tears or gaps, confirm the unit cools to temperature, inspect water and ice dispenser lines for damage, and verify the interior shelving and drawers are intact.

**How much can I save?** Typically 30–50% off retail. French door and counter-depth models often carry the steepest discounts.

**What refrigerator type has the best deals?** French door refrigerators. They're the most common type to get damaged during delivery and command the steepest discounts.

**Can I get a manufacturer warranty?** Sometimes. Ask the retailer whether the original warranty transfers. Many manufacturers honor the warranty on units with cosmetic-only damage.
