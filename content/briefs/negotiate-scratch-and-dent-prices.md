## Research Brief

### Primary Question
How can you negotiate better prices on scratch and dent appliances?

### Target Reader
- Budget shoppers who want to maximize every dollar on a major appliance purchase
- First-time negotiators who feel uncomfortable haggling and want a framework
- Experienced bargain hunters looking for scratch-and-dent-specific tactics
- Anyone buying multiple appliances who wants to leverage bundle deals

### Why This Matters
- Scratch and dent sticker prices are often a starting point, not a final offer
- Strategic timing, specific damage documentation, and bundling can unlock 10-25% additional savings
- Most shoppers leave money on the table because they don't realize negotiation is expected at many retailers
- Even when price won't budge, free delivery, installation, or accessories add $75-$300 in value

### Key Findings
- Documenting specific damage with photos and measurements gives you concrete leverage to justify a lower price
- End-of-month visits align with sales quotas, giving staff more motivation to close deals
- Bundling two or more scratch and dent appliances increases leverage significantly — stores prefer moving multiple units at once
- Asking for value-adds (free delivery, haul-away, accessories) often succeeds when direct price cuts are refused
- Local and outlet stores offer the most flexibility; big box stores have moderate flexibility through manager discretion
- Units that have been on the floor for 30+ days have the most negotiation room — managers want to free floor space

### Common Misconceptions
- "Big box stores never negotiate" — managers at major retailers do have discretion, especially on scratch and dent inventory that's been sitting
- "Negotiating is rude or pushy" — polite, informed negotiation is expected at appliance stores and appreciated by salespeople who earn commission
- "The sticker price is the best you'll get" — sticker prices on scratch and dent are often marked down once and can be reduced further

### Scope Boundaries
This research explicitly does NOT cover:
- Online-only negotiation or coupon stacking strategies
- Financing or payment plan negotiations
- Specific retailer policies or price-match guarantees

### Risk & Caution Notes
- Never sacrifice warranty documentation or return policy protections for a lower price
- If a deal seems too good, verify the unit hasn't been refurbished or returned — true scratch and dent is new and unused
- Be prepared to walk away; desperation eliminates all leverage
