## Research Brief

### Primary Question
What should I know before buying a scratch and dent range or oven?

### Target Reader
- Homeowners replacing an aging range or oven on a budget
- Kitchen remodelers looking to save on cooking appliances
- First-time homebuyers furnishing a kitchen affordably
- Landlords equipping rental units with functional ranges

### Why This Matters
- **High savings potential:** Ranges retail $800-$3,000+, so scratch and dent savings of 30-50% are significant
- **Safety considerations:** Gas ranges and high-voltage electric ranges require extra inspection attention
- **Installation hides damage:** Most range dents are on sides and back panels, invisible once installed between cabinets
- **Functional risks differ from cosmetic:** Door seal integrity and burner alignment are critical checkpoints

### Key Findings
- Scratch and dent ranges typically save $250-$1,200 depending on the original price point and extent of cosmetic damage
- Side panel dents are the most common damage type and are completely hidden once a range is installed between countertops
- Gas ranges require checking all burner ignitions, gas connection fittings, and ensuring no damage near gas inlet areas
- Electric and induction ranges need verification that all heating elements cycle on and off properly and that glass cooktops have no cracks
- Oven door seals are critical — a compromised seal wastes energy and causes uneven cooking; check for gaps or warping
- Slide-in ranges hide more damage than freestanding models since they sit flush with cabinetry on three sides

### Common Misconceptions
- "A dented range won't cook properly" — FALSE: Cosmetic dents on exterior panels have zero effect on cooking performance
- "Gas ranges are too risky to buy scratch and dent" — FALSE: Gas connections and valves are internal components rarely affected by cosmetic shipping damage
- "You need a professional to inspect a scratch and dent range" — FALSE: A basic visual and functional check (burners, oven heat, door seal) covers the key risks

### Scope Boundaries
This research explicitly does NOT cover:
- Brand-specific range recommendations or comparisons
- Installation instructions for gas or electric ranges
- Commercial or professional-grade range considerations

### Risk & Caution Notes
- Never purchase a gas range with visible damage near the gas inlet or connection point — even minor dents in that area warrant professional inspection
- Confirm the range matches your kitchen's power supply (gas vs. 240V electric) before purchasing
- Ask about delivery and old-unit haul-away costs, as ranges are heavy and require careful handling
