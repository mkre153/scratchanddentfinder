## Research Brief

### Primary Question
Is buying scratch and dent appliances a smart move for rental properties?

### Target Reader
- Landlords furnishing single or multi-unit rental properties
- Property managers replacing appliances across portfolios
- Real estate investors calculating rehab budgets for flips or rentals
- First-time landlords equipping their first rental unit

### Why This Matters
- **Direct ROI impact:** Appliance packages for a rental unit cost $2,000-$5,000 retail; scratch and dent cuts that by 30-50%
- **Scale multiplier:** Savings compound across multiple units — 10 units at $1,500 savings each = $15,000
- **Tenant expectations are realistic:** Most tenants care about function, not cosmetics
- **Warranty coverage still applies:** Full manufacturer warranties protect landlords the same as retail purchases

### Key Findings
- A typical rental unit needs 4-5 appliances (fridge, range, dishwasher, microwave, washer/dryer); scratch and dent can save $1,000-$2,500 per unit
- Scratch and dent appliances carry the same manufacturer warranty as retail — typically 1 year parts and labor
- Most tenant complaints relate to appliance function, not appearance; cosmetic dents rarely generate maintenance calls
- Bulk buying from scratch and dent dealers or liquidators can yield additional 10-15% volume discounts
- White and black finishes hide minor scratches better than stainless steel, a practical choice for rentals
- Some scratch and dent dealers offer landlord programs with delivery, installation, and haul-away bundled

### Common Misconceptions
- "Tenants will complain about dented appliances" — FALSE: Once installed, most cosmetic damage is invisible; tenants care that appliances work
- "Scratch and dent appliances break down faster" — FALSE: The damage is cosmetic only; internal components are identical to retail units
- "You can't get matching sets scratch and dent" — FALSE: Many dealers and outlets carry full kitchen packages

### Scope Boundaries
This research explicitly does NOT cover:
- Landlord-tenant law regarding appliance requirements by state
- Tax depreciation strategies for rental appliances
- Specific dealer or liquidator recommendations

### Risk & Caution Notes
- Document the condition of every scratch and dent appliance with photos before tenant move-in for lease protection
- Verify warranty transferability if you sell the property — most manufacturer warranties follow the appliance, not the owner
- Budget for delivery and installation costs, which may not be included with scratch and dent purchases from smaller dealers
