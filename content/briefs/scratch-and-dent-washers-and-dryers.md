## Research Brief

### Primary Question
What should you know before buying scratch and dent washers and dryers?

### Target Reader
- Homeowners replacing an aging washer, dryer, or both
- First-time buyers furnishing a home or apartment
- Landlords purchasing laundry equipment for rental units
- Budget shoppers comparing front-load vs top-load options

### Why This Matters
- A washer-dryer set typically costs $1,200-$2,500 at retail; scratch and dent saves $300-$800 on the pair
- Laundry appliances sit in closets, utility rooms, or garages where side and top damage is completely hidden
- Buying a matched set in scratch and dent lets you bundle for additional negotiation leverage
- Front-load units are more prone to transit dents on the door, making inspection especially important

### Key Findings
- Top-load washers are the safest scratch and dent buy — the lid hides top damage and sides are always concealed
- Front-load washers require door seal inspection; a warped door from impact can cause leaks over time
- Dryers have fewer moving parts than washers, making cosmetic-only damage even more likely to stay cosmetic
- Stacking kits work fine on scratch and dent units as long as the top surface is flat and not structurally dented
- Buying a mismatched pair (different cosmetic damage, same brand) can save more than waiting for a matched set
- Gas dryer connections should be inspected for kinks in the gas line area even on units with only external dents

### Common Misconceptions
- "Front-load washers are risky scratch and dent buys" — only if the door or door gasket is damaged; side and top dents are irrelevant
- "You need a matching set" — mismatched colors or slightly different models from the same brand work identically and save more
- "Scratch and dent washers vibrate more" — vibration is caused by leveling and load balance, not by cosmetic dents on the cabinet

### Scope Boundaries
This research explicitly does NOT cover:
- Brand-specific washer or dryer recommendations
- Ventless or combo washer-dryer units (niche category)
- Commercial laundry equipment

### Risk & Caution Notes
- Always run a spin cycle test if the store allows — listen for grinding or banging that could indicate drum damage
- On front-loaders, check that the door closes flush and the rubber boot gasket has no tears or warping
- For stacking units, confirm the top panel is flat and not buckled; a warped top panel can make stacking unstable
