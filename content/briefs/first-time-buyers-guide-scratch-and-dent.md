## Research Brief

### Primary Question
How do I buy scratch and dent appliances for the first time without making expensive mistakes?

### Target Reader
- People who have never purchased a scratch and dent appliance before
- Budget-conscious consumers considering scratch and dent for the first time
- Young adults furnishing their first apartment or home
- Anyone intimidated by the scratch and dent buying process

### Why This Matters
- **Knowledge gap costs money:** First-timers often accept sticker price without negotiating, missing 10-25% additional savings
- **Inspection anxiety:** Not knowing what to check leads to either bad purchases or walking away from great deals
- **Warranty confusion:** Many first-timers wrongly assume scratch and dent means no warranty
- **Process unfamiliarity:** The buying process differs from retail and can feel overwhelming without guidance

### Key Findings
- Scratch and dent appliances are new, unused units with cosmetic damage from shipping, warehousing, or display — they are not used or refurbished
- Full manufacturer warranties (typically 1 year parts and labor) come standard with scratch and dent purchases from authorized dealers
- The step-by-step process: research retail price, visit stores, inspect the unit, test functionality, negotiate, confirm warranty and return policy, arrange delivery
- Savings typically range from 20-50% off retail price, with an average of about 30% on most major appliances
- The most important inspection points: door seals, control panels, interior condition, and making sure the unit powers on
- Return policies vary widely — some stores offer 24-48 hour return windows, others sell strictly as-is

### Common Misconceptions
- "Scratch and dent means used" — FALSE: These are brand-new appliances with cosmetic imperfections only
- "You can't return scratch and dent appliances" — FALSE: Many retailers offer short return windows; always ask before purchasing
- "The savings aren't worth the hassle" — FALSE: Average savings of $300-$1,000+ per appliance with minimal extra effort

### Risk & Caution Notes
- Always ask about the return policy before purchasing — get it in writing if possible
- Bring a phone to photograph the appliance, damage areas, model number, and serial number for your records
- Measure your space before shopping — scratch and dent inventory is limited and exchanges are often not possible
