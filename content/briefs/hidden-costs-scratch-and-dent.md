## Research Brief

### Primary Question
What hidden costs should I watch out for when buying scratch and dent appliances?

### Target Reader
- First-time scratch and dent buyers who want to budget accurately
- Savvy shoppers who want to compare true total cost vs. retail
- Homeowners on a tight renovation budget who can't afford surprises
- Landlords calculating the real per-unit appliance cost

### Why This Matters
- **Delivery fees add up:** Scratch and dent stores often charge $75-$150+ for delivery, while many retail purchases include free delivery
- **Missing accessories cost real money:** Shelves, ice maker kits, power cords, and drain hoses can add $30-$200 per item
- **No-return policies mean no safety net:** A bad purchase from an as-is dealer has no recourse
- **Energy efficiency gaps:** Older or less efficient models may cost more to operate over their lifetime

### Key Findings
- Delivery fees for scratch and dent appliances typically range from $50-$150 per item, compared to free delivery often included with retail purchases
- Missing accessories are common: refrigerator shelves ($40-$80), ice maker kits ($80-$120), range power cords ($20-$40), dishwasher drain hoses ($15-$30)
- Installation costs for gas ranges ($100-$200), dishwashers ($100-$175), and built-in microwaves ($75-$150) are often separate from delivery
- Haul-away of the old appliance adds $25-$75 per unit at many scratch and dent dealers
- Energy efficiency differences between scratch and dent (often prior-year models) and current ENERGY STAR models can mean $30-$80 per year in operating costs
- Extended warranty purchases ($100-$300) may be worth considering since scratch and dent appliances sometimes have cosmetic damage that makes future service calls harder to diagnose

### Common Misconceptions
- "Scratch and dent is always cheaper than retail after all costs" — NOT ALWAYS: Once you add delivery, accessories, and installation, a sale-priced retail unit can occasionally beat scratch and dent on total cost
- "All scratch and dent stores charge the same fees" — FALSE: Fees vary widely; some dealers include delivery and installation in the price while others charge separately for each service
- "Energy costs don't matter for appliances" — FALSE: A less efficient refrigerator can cost $50-$80 more per year to run than an ENERGY STAR certified model

### Scope Boundaries
This research explicitly does NOT cover:
- Specific fee schedules for any retailer
- Extended warranty comparisons between providers
- Appliance repair costs after warranty expiration

### Risk & Caution Notes
- Always ask for an itemized total including delivery, installation, haul-away, and accessories before committing
- Check EnergyGuide labels to estimate annual operating costs — a cheap purchase price can be offset by high energy bills
- Confirm whether the unit includes all standard accessories (shelves, racks, cords, hoses) before leaving the store
