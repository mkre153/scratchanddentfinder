## Research Brief

### Primary Question
What is the difference between scratch and dent and open box appliances, and which one is the better buy?

### Target Reader
- Shoppers browsing discount appliance sections at big-box stores
- People who see both labels online and don't know what they mean
- Budget buyers trying to maximize savings without increasing risk

### Why This Matters
- The two categories have different origins, conditions, and price points
- Open box may have been used briefly; scratch and dent has never been used
- Warranty coverage can differ significantly between the two

### Key Findings
- Scratch and dent appliances are brand-new units that sustained cosmetic damage during manufacturing, shipping, or warehousing but were never sold or used by a consumer
- Open box appliances were purchased by a consumer, unboxed, and returned — they may have been used for days or weeks before being sent back
- Scratch and dent typically saves 30-60% off retail; open box typically saves 15-30% off retail
- Scratch and dent nearly always retains the full manufacturer warranty since the unit was never activated by a consumer
- Open box warranty status depends on whether the original buyer registered the product — if registered, the warranty clock has already started
- Open box appliances may include all original accessories and packaging; scratch and dent may be missing original packaging

### Common Misconceptions
- "Open box and scratch and dent are the same thing" - They have fundamentally different histories and condition profiles
- "Open box is always cheaper" - Scratch and dent often offers deeper discounts despite being in unused condition
- "Open box appliances are like new" - They may have been used, and internal wear is harder to detect than cosmetic damage

### Scope Boundaries
This research explicitly does NOT cover:
- Refurbished or reconditioned appliance comparisons (covered in separate brief)
- Specific retailer pricing or inventory
- Floor model or display unit comparisons

### Risk & Caution Notes
- For open box, ask whether the unit was ever powered on or connected to water/gas lines
- Check warranty registration status on open box units before purchasing
- Inspect both types in person whenever possible — photos can hide or exaggerate damage
