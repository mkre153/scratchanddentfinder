## Research Brief

### Primary Question
When are the best times throughout the year to find the deepest discounts and widest selection of scratch and dent appliances?

### Target Reader
- Patient shoppers who can wait for the right time to buy
- Homeowners planning a renovation who want to time appliance purchases strategically
- Deal hunters who want to stack seasonal savings with scratch and dent discounts

### Why This Matters
- Timing a purchase correctly can mean an extra 10-20% in savings on top of standard scratch and dent discounts
- Inventory levels fluctuate throughout the year based on new model releases and shipping volumes
- Holiday weekends create predictable pricing patterns that repeat annually

### Key Findings
- September through November is the prime scratch and dent season — manufacturers release new models in fall, and retailers aggressively discount outgoing inventory including damaged units
- Holiday weekends (Presidents' Day, Memorial Day, July 4th, Labor Day, Black Friday) are traditional appliance sale events where scratch and dent gets additional markdowns
- January is strong for scratch and dent because retailers clear holiday overstock and returns from the gifting season
- Late spring (April-May) sees increased inventory as retailers receive shipments damaged during seasonal logistics surges
- Black Friday through Cyber Monday offers some of the year's deepest discounts, but scratch and dent selection can be picked over quickly
- Summer (June-August) is generally the slowest period with less inventory and fewer promotions, though stores may negotiate more to move existing stock

### Common Misconceptions
- "Black Friday has the best scratch and dent deals" - Selection is often limited; September-October new-model transitions offer better overall value
- "Scratch and dent pricing is the same year-round" - Seasonal inventory fluctuations create real pricing swings of 10-20%
- "You should wait for a sale to buy scratch and dent" - If you find the right unit at a good price, waiting risks losing it since scratch and dent items are one-of-a-kind

### Scope Boundaries
This research explicitly does NOT cover:
- Specific retailer sale calendars or promotional schedules
- Online-only deal timing (flash sales, deal-of-the-day sites)
- Used or refurbished appliance seasonal patterns

### Risk & Caution Notes
- Scratch and dent items are one-of-a-kind — waiting for a better price means risking someone else buying it
- Holiday weekend "doorbuster" pricing may apply only to specific units, not all scratch and dent inventory
- Factor in the cost of living without the appliance if your current one is failing — waiting for a deal may not be worth it
