## Research Brief

### Primary Question
Are scratch and dent appliances safe to use in your home, and what should you check before buying?

### Target Reader
- Parents and homeowners worried about appliance safety
- First-time scratch and dent buyers with safety concerns
- Anyone who has heard horror stories about discounted appliances

### Why This Matters
- Safety fears prevent people from saving hundreds of dollars on perfectly good appliances
- Scratch and dent units undergo the same factory testing as full-price models
- Knowing what to inspect removes guesswork and builds confidence

### Key Findings
- Scratch and dent appliances are manufactured on the same production lines and pass the same UL (Underwriters Laboratories) and ETL safety certifications as full-price units
- Cosmetic damage like dents, scratches, and paint chips does not affect internal components, wiring, gas lines, or safety systems
- Major manufacturers including Whirlpool, GE, Samsung, and LG confirm that cosmetic damage does not void safety certifications
- The only safety risk comes from structural damage that compromises seals (refrigerator door gaskets) or ventilation (dryer exhaust connections)
- Retailers are required to disclose any known functional defects; cosmetic-only damage does not constitute a functional defect
- Gas appliances should always be professionally installed regardless of whether they are new, scratch and dent, or refurbished

### Common Misconceptions
- "A dent means something is broken inside" - External cosmetic damage almost never affects internal components
- "Scratch and dent appliances are recalled units" - They are standard production units with shipping damage, not safety recalls
- "Discount appliances skip safety testing" - All appliances sold in the US must meet the same CPSC and UL/ETL standards

### Scope Boundaries
This research explicitly does NOT cover:
- Recalled appliance lookup procedures (separate topic)
- Refurbished or used appliance safety concerns
- Installation safety beyond general guidance

### Risk & Caution Notes
- Always check the CPSC recall database (cpsc.gov) for any appliance before purchase, regardless of condition
- Inspect door seals, hinges, and ventilation openings for damage that could affect performance
- Have gas appliances professionally installed and tested for leaks
