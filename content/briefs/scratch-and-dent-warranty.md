## Research Brief

### Primary Question
Do scratch and dent appliances come with a warranty?

### Target Reader
- First-time scratch and dent shoppers worried about being stuck with a broken appliance
- Cautious buyers who want peace of mind before spending $500-$2,000
- Anyone comparing scratch and dent to refurbished or open-box and wondering how warranty differs

### Why This Matters
- Warranty uncertainty is the single biggest barrier to scratch and dent purchases
- Most scratch and dent units are brand-new and carry full manufacturer warranties identical to retail
- Understanding warranty types (manufacturer, store, extended) helps shoppers make confident decisions
- Knowing red flags prevents buyers from accidentally purchasing units with voided coverage

### Key Findings
- Manufacturer warranties typically cover scratch and dent appliances identically to retail units — 1 year parts and labor is standard
- Extended components (compressors, sealed systems, motors) often carry 5-10 year manufacturer warranties regardless of cosmetic condition
- Store warranties and return policies vary significantly; some outlets offer 30-day exchanges while others sell as-is
- Extended warranty plans from third-party providers generally cover scratch and dent units the same as new, but always confirm before purchasing
- The warranty clock starts at purchase date (or delivery date for some brands), not manufacture date
- Authorized dealers are more likely to honor warranties smoothly than unauthorized resellers or liquidators

### Common Misconceptions
- "Scratch and dent means no warranty" — false; the vast majority carry full manufacturer coverage since they are new, unused units
- "Cosmetic damage voids the warranty" — false; manufacturers distinguish between cosmetic and functional defects; cosmetic damage does not void functional coverage
- "Extended warranties aren't available for scratch and dent" — false; most third-party plans cover them, though some require proof the unit was sold as new

### Scope Boundaries
This research explicitly does NOT cover:
- Specific manufacturer warranty terms (varies by brand and model)
- Extended warranty plan comparisons or recommendations
- Refurbished appliance warranty coverage (different category)

### Risk & Caution Notes
- Always get warranty documentation in writing before leaving the store — verbal promises are unenforceable
- Verify the unit's serial number matches the warranty card; mismatched serials can complicate claims
- Be cautious with liquidation or clearance outlets that sell "as-is" with no warranty; these are the exception, not the norm
