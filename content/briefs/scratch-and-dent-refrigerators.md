## Research Brief

### Primary Question
Are scratch and dent refrigerators worth buying, and what should you look for?

### Target Reader
- Homeowners upgrading a kitchen on a budget
- First-time buyers furnishing a new home or apartment
- Landlords purchasing rental-property appliances
- Anyone replacing a broken fridge who needs a deal fast

### Why This Matters
- Refrigerators have the highest retail prices of any kitchen appliance, making dollar savings the largest in scratch and dent
- French door models regularly retail $2,000-$4,000, so even a 25% scratch and dent discount saves $500-$1,000
- Side panels and backs are hidden once installed, meaning most cosmetic damage is invisible in your kitchen
- Buyers often overpay for perfect condition when the damage would never be seen

### Key Findings
- French door refrigerators offer the deepest dollar savings ($400-$1,200 off) due to high retail prices
- Side-by-side models are excellent scratch and dent buys because both side panels are hidden between cabinets and walls
- Top-freezer models have the lowest retail prices, so scratch and dent savings are smaller ($100-$300) but still worthwhile
- Door dents matter most because they remain visible; side, back, and top dents are hidden after installation
- Compressor and sealed-system components are almost never affected by cosmetic shipping damage
- Full manufacturer warranties (typically 1 year parts and labor, 5-10 years on compressor) apply to scratch and dent units

### Common Misconceptions
- "A dented fridge won't cool properly" — cosmetic dents on exterior panels have zero effect on refrigeration performance
- "Scratch and dent refrigerators are used or returned" — true scratch and dent units are brand-new, never powered on, damaged only during shipping or warehousing
- "You can't get high-end brands in scratch and dent" — premium brands are actually more common because their larger size leads to more transit damage

### Scope Boundaries
This research explicitly does NOT cover:
- Brand-specific model recommendations or rankings
- Smart refrigerator features and connectivity
- Refurbished or open-box refrigerators (different category)

### Risk & Caution Notes
- Always verify door gaskets seal properly — a poor seal increases energy costs and spoils food
- Listen for the compressor during a test run; rattling or clicking may indicate internal damage beyond cosmetics
- Check ice maker and water dispenser lines if equipped — kinked lines during shipping can cause leaks after install
