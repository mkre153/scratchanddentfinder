## Research Brief

### Primary Question
Are scratch and dent dishwashers worth buying?

### Target Reader
- Homeowners replacing an old or broken dishwasher on a budget
- Kitchen remodelers looking to save on appliances without sacrificing quality
- First-time homebuyers furnishing a kitchen
- Landlords outfitting rental properties

### Why This Matters
- Dishwashers are installed under countertops with only the door panel visible, hiding all top, side, and back damage
- Many premium dishwashers accept custom cabinet panels, hiding even the door entirely
- Savings of $100-$500 on a $500-$1,200 appliance represent 20-40% off retail
- Dishwashers are one of the most frequently damaged appliances during shipping due to their lightweight sheet-metal construction

### Key Findings
- Only the door front is visible once installed; top, sides, and back are completely hidden by countertop and cabinets
- Panel-ready dishwashers hide even the door behind a cabinet panel, making any cosmetic damage 100% invisible
- Scratch and dent dishwashers typically save $100-$500 depending on brand and model tier
- Interior rack condition is the most important thing to inspect — bent tines or warped racks affect daily usability
- Spray arms should spin freely; a bent spray arm from rough handling reduces cleaning performance
- Door latch alignment matters — if the door doesn't close flush, the dishwasher will leak during cycles

### Common Misconceptions
- "Dishwashers aren't worth buying scratch and dent because savings are small" — on mid-range and premium models ($800-$1,200 retail), savings of $200-$500 are significant
- "A dented dishwasher won't fit in the cabinet opening" — standard openings accommodate slight exterior dents; only severe structural warping causes fit issues
- "Interior damage is common on scratch and dent dishwashers" — most transit damage affects exterior panels, not the stainless interior tub

### Scope Boundaries
This research explicitly does NOT cover:
- Portable or countertop dishwasher models
- Brand-specific model recommendations
- Installation guides or plumbing requirements

### Risk & Caution Notes
- Always test the door latch before buying — a misaligned latch causes leaks that can damage kitchen flooring
- Check the tub interior for dents or cracks; even small cracks in a plastic tub can lead to leaks over time
- Verify that the power cord and water inlet valve area are undamaged, as these are occasionally affected by bottom-panel impacts
