## Research Brief

### Primary Question
How much can you realistically save on scratch and dent appliances compared to buying new at retail price?

### Target Reader
- Budget-conscious homeowners planning a kitchen or laundry renovation
- First-time buyers wondering if scratch and dent savings are worth the trade-offs
- Anyone comparing discount options to stretch their appliance budget further

### Why This Matters
- Knowing real savings ranges helps shoppers set realistic expectations
- Savings vary dramatically by appliance type, damage grade, and retailer
- Understanding what drives the discount helps negotiate better prices

### Key Findings
- Overall scratch and dent savings typically range from 20-70% off MSRP, with most deals falling in the 30-50% range
- Refrigerators: 25-50% off retail ($300-$1,200 savings on units retailing $1,000-$3,000)
- Washers and dryers: 30-50% off retail ($200-$600 savings per unit)
- Dishwashers: 25-40% off retail ($150-$400 savings)
- Ranges and ovens: 30-55% off retail ($250-$800 savings)
- Damage grade is the biggest price factor: Grade A (barely noticeable) saves 20-30%, Grade C/D (visible dents) can save 50-70%
- Stacking scratch and dent with holiday sales, floor model clearances, or store closing events can push savings above 60%
- Premium and luxury brands often see the deepest dollar savings since the baseline price is higher

### Common Misconceptions
- "You only save 10-15% on scratch and dent" - Savings regularly exceed 30% and can reach 70% on heavily damaged cosmetic units
- "The best deals are online" - In-store scratch and dent often offers deeper discounts since shipping damaged items is costly for retailers
- "All scratch and dent is priced the same" - Pricing varies significantly based on the severity and location of the damage

### Scope Boundaries
This research explicitly does NOT cover:
- Specific retailer pricing or current promotions
- Refurbished or used appliance pricing comparisons
- Financing options for appliance purchases

### Risk & Caution Notes
- Advertised "percentage off" may be calculated from inflated MSRP rather than typical street price
- Factor in delivery fees when calculating total savings — some scratch and dent stores charge more for delivery
- Compare the scratch and dent price against actual sale prices at competing retailers, not just MSRP
